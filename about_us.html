KTP at a Glance

Our Story

Kappa Theta Pi is a distinguished co-ed professional Technology Fraternity, is dedicated to the promotion and advancement of technology-centric careers amongst collegiate students. Our fraternity is a nurturing hub for those who share a passion for technology, bridging the gap between academics and industry. We offer an inspiring environment where students can cultivate their technical skills, gain industry insights, and network with like-minded individuals, tech leaders, and alumni. Our mission is to empower our members, equipping them with the tools, resources, and support they need to excel in their chosen tech career. Whether it's coding, AI, cybersecurity, or any other tech-related field, our fraternity is committed to fostering innovation, excellence, and camaraderie, with the ultimate goal of aiding our members in landing their dream tech job.

Our History

THIS CONTAINS INCORRECT INFORMATION!!! Kappa Theta Pi was founded in 2015 by a group of students at the University of Colorado Boulder. The founders envisioned a fraternity that would serve as a platform for students to explore their passion for technology and gain the skills and knowledge they need to succeed in the tech industry. The fraternity was officially recognized by the University of Colorado Boulder in 2016. Since then, Kappa Theta Pi has grown to become one of the most prominent fraternities on campus, with over 100 active members and a strong alumni network. Our fraternity has also expanded to other universities, including the University of California, Berkeley, and the University of California, Los Angeles. Kappa Theta Pi is a proud member of the Professional Fraternity Association (PFA), a national organization that represents over 100,000 students.
